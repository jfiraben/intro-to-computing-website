Background

So how does this technology work? How does a small camera go from taking a picture of someoneâ€™s eye to matching that with a database on that person? According to Kaur et al. (2010), the process can be described as:

Iris recognition analyzes features like rings, furrows, and freckles existing in the colored tissue surrounding the pupil. Image processing techniques can be employed to extract the unique iris pattern from a digitized image of the eye, and encode it into a biometric template, which can be stored in a database. This biometric template contains an objective mathematical representation of the unique information stored in the iris, and allows comparisons to be made between templates.

In much simpler terms, the camera takes a picture of the eye, focuses in on just the tissue surrounding the pupil, then compares the unique features from that picture with a database on the person.
